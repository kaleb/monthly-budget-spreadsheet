Note: This article is slightly out of date and is in the process of being updated. The screenshots are old, but I have made some new ones.


Right now, I am taking a course from Financial Peace University. They had some nice budgeting PDF files, but I thought they would be better suited as spreadsheets. The following is the result.

I used Google Docs for my spreadsheet, because I can access it anywhere I have an internet connection. In addition, Google Docs has a nice forms feature that makes inputing data easy from both my phone and my computer.

I use the form to input all my cash expenses so that I can keep track of how much I am spending. When a form is submitted, the data gets automatically and (almost) instantaneously inserted into my spreadsheet along with a timestamp of when the data was entered. In this form, I made the category field a dropdown menu to select from to make input from my phone quicker, easier, and more consistent due to less typos from manual input. If one wants to change the categories, it is very easy to do.

Another sheet is the Cash Flow sheet. Like in many of the other sheets, all cells in yellow are for editing by hand, all the others are labels or will be calculated automatically. Some of the cells that will be calculated automatically are the cells in the Grocery and Restaurant rows under the Actually Spent column. All items entered in the form will be totaled for their corresponding category, thanks to the SUMIF spreadsheet function.

The Total column is where the total planned budget allotment is entered at the first of the month. If you only want to spend $200 on groceries this month, put 200 in the proper field.

The % Income field shows the planned budget item percent of total Income. To see or change what this month's total income is, head over to the Income sheet. This sheet is for inputting regular income. Dave Ramsey has a form for inputting irregular income, but since it does not apply to me, I did not add it, but I may upon popular demand.

To input more detail for the individual weeks of the month is the Spending Plan sheet. For each week column, just input how much you plan to spend. It may make things easier if the title Week 1 was changed to June 1 or something similar.

Finally, if you do not know how much percentage of your monthly income should go to what, there is a handy Instructions sheet to help you out that shows Dave Ramsey's recommended percentages.

To get this template, just go to http://go.hornsby.ws/budget-template and follow the instructions at the top. Happy Budgeting.
